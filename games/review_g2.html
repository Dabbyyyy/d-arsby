Review: Elden Ring - Shadow of the Erdtree

Even the DLC surpasses ninety-nine percent of games out there

Video Game 2022 Masterpiece

As a long-time fan of FromSoftware's titles, Elden Ring was a revelation. It took the punishing yet rewarding formula of the Souls series and expanded it into a vast, open world teeming with mystery, danger, and beauty. The Lands Between felt alive, each corner hiding secrets, formidable foes, and stories waiting to be uncovered.

The freedom to explore at my own pace, to stumble upon a hidden cave or a towering boss, made every session unpredictable and exhilarating. The combat was tight and responsive, offering a plethora of weapons and spells to suit various playstyles. The lore, co-crafted by George R.R. Martin, added depth to the world, making every discovery feel significant.

When Shadow of the Erdtree was announced, my anticipation soared. Returning to this world with new challenges and stories was an opportunity I couldn't pass up.

Shadow of the Erdtree doesn't just add content; it enriches the Elden Ring experience. The new region, the Land of Shadow, is both haunting and mesmerizing. From the eerie Cerulean Coast to the treacherous Ancient Ruins of Rauh, the landscapes are meticulously crafted, each with its own atmosphere and secrets.

The expansion introduces a unique leveling system through Scadutree Fragments, encouraging exploration and providing a fresh progression mechanic. The enemies are formidable, with bosses that test your skills and patience, reminiscent of the base game's most challenging encounters.

Narratively, Shadow of the Erdtree delves deeper into the lore, focusing on Miquella's journey. The storytelling is subtle yet impactful, rewarding players who pay attention to environmental cues and item descriptions.

In conclusion, Shadow of the Erdtree is a masterful expansion that builds upon the strengths of Elden Ring, offering new challenges, stories, and areas to explore. It's a testament to FromSoftware's commitment to crafting immersive and rewarding experiences.

Rating: 10/10
